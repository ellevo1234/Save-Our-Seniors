ABOUT THE SITUATION

Why we need to prevent the abuse of the elderly

What is Ageism

Ageism is the term used to describe bias or discrimination against someone based on their age although this is usually directed at older adults. It is assuming anything about someone's worth, competency, or ability based only on their age and can appear in a number of contexts, including the healthcare system, the workplace, and interpersonal relationships.

The effects of isolating seniors

It is extremely common for families to send their seniors to nursing homes although if the seniors have nobdoy else to talk to it serves to harm their social, mental and emotional wellbeing.

Where does ageism occur?

The most common type of ageism occurs in the workplace although different types of ageism can be interpersonal, self directed or institutional. Ageism is especially common in the workplace, where it can negatively impact your mental and financial well-being. Seventy-eight percent of older workers reported having witnessed or experienced age discrimination at work in a 2020 poll.

Support

It is absoloutely crucial that we their descendants treat them respect with the few decades they have left, spending time with our loved ones or some random elderly man or woman in the street can brighten their day significantly. In order to advance inclusivity and equality for individuals of all ages, ageism must be confronted and challenged.

Need Support?

  • 1. Stand up for yourself and don't let others push you around because you’re older.
  • 2. Engage in the world. Staying active both mentally and physically is great at deflecting ageism. For example, look to the future, check in with the news, live in the present.
  • 3. Be positive. Attitude has a lot to do with how people can overcome ageism, Hinrichsen says. Relish the experience and wisdom that come with age and put them to good use.
  • 4. Stay independent. Older ages are often associated with helplessness, as a result in order to combat stereotypes it is encouraged to do activites that inhabit independence.
  • 5. Stay social. This includes socialising wiht younger people, taking a class at the gym or the community college with younger people will help fight ageism or preconceived stereotypes against people.
  • 6. Join in activities or volunteer at places, such as supporting sick kids, spending time with neglected abused kids, etc.
  • 7. Physically exercise. It can be as intense as heavy lifting or pilates or it can be simple exercises like walking.

Places and events for the Elderly

  • There is a program that takes place in Campbelltown called Café Connect hosting a number of gatherings where you can have a bite or a drink while chatting with new people. There is also a lot of information at the Café Connect events to assist you in making connections with neighbourhood social organisations, home help, and other agencies. Each café is unique, and you are welcome to participate in the events or just stop by for a conversation. This last took in place April-June 2021.
  • The Seniors Festival 2023 was the largest opportunity for people over 60 to socialise. It's also for those who are 50 years of age or older and are of Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander background, or who have a lifelong handicap. This program ran in Wednesday 1 February to Sunday 12 February 2023.
  • Macarthur Heights Walking Group is a great way for the elderly to exercise with other old people too. This is not limited by age and currently there have 26 members, walk weekly at a slow pace for 30-45 minutes at Tuesday 6:30pm and Thursday 6:30pm. Their Website:
